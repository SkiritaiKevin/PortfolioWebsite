Generative AI & Security

Summer research with Dr. Yang Wang exploring how tools like ChatGPT and Gemini transform ethical hacking workflows, from reconnaisance to reporting.

Overview

This project began in the summer of 2024 and continued through the academic year. We investigated the practical impact of generative AI on information security and ethical hacking practice, focusing on both opportunities and risks for professionals and students.

GenAI Security Ethical Hacking Education

Research Focus

  • Analyzed ChatGPT and Gemini in security-relevant tasks.
  • Defined methods for AI jailbreaking, social engineering, and automation of programming/scripting tasks.
  • Mapped GenAI across ethical hacking phases: reconnaisance, scanning, gaining/maintaining access, covering tracks, and reporting.

Methods & Outcomes

  • Experiment design for safe prompt engineering and red-teaming.
  • Creation of reproducible labs for classroom use.
  • Documentation of risks/mitigations (guardrails, verification, logging).

Publication

Y. Wang, K. Beideman, M. McCoey, and M. Jalalitabar, "Empowering Ethical Hacking Education with Generative AIs: A ChatGPT-Infused Curriculum," Proc. 2025 IEEE Integrated STEM Education Conference, pp. 1-7.
